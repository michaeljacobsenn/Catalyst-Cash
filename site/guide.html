App Map

What each area of the app is for.

The product works best when you know which surface answers which question.

Dashboard

Use it for the current snapshot: health score, safe-to-spend, trend cards, and the fastest read on whether the week improved.

Audit

Use it for the weekly decision pass. It packages cash pressure, debt focus, renewals, and portfolio context into a readable action list.

AskAI

Use it for tradeoffs after the audit: affordability, debt sequencing, timing decisions, and “what if I do this instead?”

Portfolio Vault

Use it to manage banks, cards, loans, investments, crypto, other assets, and transaction previews in one place.

Cashflow + Renewals

Use these surfaces to manage budget envelopes, recurring charges, payment timing, and the 30-day cash-flow picture.

History + Ledger

Use History to compare old audits. Use the Pro ledger when you need search, filtering, export, and transaction-level cleanup.

Weekly Workflow

The operating loop that makes the app useful.

Most value comes from repetition, not from a one-time setup sprint.

01

Refresh the numbers

Sync through Plaid or update balances manually so the audit is working from current reality.

02

Run the audit

Start with the week color, safe-to-spend logic, debt priority, and the top action list.

03

Open the supporting tool

Use AskAI for the tradeoff, Renewals for recurring costs, Budget for pacing, or the ledger for cleanup.

Financial Logic

The main rules the product cares about.

FLR

Checking floor first

The audit treats your emergency floor as a hard safety boundary. If the floor is dishonest, the advice will be too.

APR

Avalanche debt logic

The debt simulator prioritizes high APR debt to minimize interest drag, then lets you test the effect of extra payments.

RNW

Renewals are future cash claims

Recurring charges matter because of timing, not just total cost. Renewal pressure is part of weekly safety, not an afterthought.

TRN

History exists to validate decisions

Audit history should help you see whether your actions improved liquidity, reduced risk, or changed the trajectory.

Privacy

Where the data lives and what leaves the device.

The shortest accurate version is local-first by default, networked only when you use networked features.

Stored locally first

Balances, debts, renewals, settings, and audit history are kept on your device as the primary record.
